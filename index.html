Where these benchmarks come from

The cases are public benchmark families from kinetic theory and rarefied gas dynamics: exact homogeneous Boltzmann relaxation, free-molecular wall flows, molecular aperture/effusion laws, collisionless transport, Riemann fans, phase mixing, and gas-surface reflection laws.

Representative public origins include the Bobylev-Krook-Wu exact Boltzmann solution (Krook and Wu, Bobylev), Rayleigh's impulsively moved wall problem in kinetic theory (Caltech thesis record), Knudsen cosine-law emission (cosine-law discussion), and Maxwell diffuse/specular wall models used throughout rarefied-gas literature (example reference).

How to read the error numbers

Max error is the largest absolute difference between the computed benchmark quantities and the public exact/reference values for that case. A smaller number means closer agreement.

Worst max error is the largest card-level max error on this page. P95 max error means 95% of the listed cases are at or below that error level. Median max error is the middle case after sorting by error.

Decimal digits is an approximate readability label: an error near 1e-13 corresponds to about 13 decimal digits of agreement. Values shown as 0.00e+0 mean the rounded public statistic is at floating-point display precision for the checked quantities.
